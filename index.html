FastCapture: Fast Reconstruction and Rendering of Neural Humans

Ignacio Rocco, Iurii Makarov, Filippos Kokkinos, David Novotny, Benjamin Graham, Natalia Neverova, Andrea Vedaldi
Meta AI
Paper arXiv Video Code

Abstract

We present a method for fast 3D reconstruction and rendering of dynamic humans from monocular videos with accompanying parametric body fits. Our method can train in less than 3h using a single GPU, compared to recent state-of-the-art methods, which take up to 72h for training. These speedups are obtained by using a lightweight deformation model based on linear blend skinning, and an efficient factorized volumetric representation for modeling the shape and color of the person, in canonical pose.

Moreover, we propose a rasterization-guided local raymarching which allows to render the neural human on a mobile VR device at a 40 FPS framerate. Our experimental evaluation shows superior or competitive results with state-of-the art methods, despite the large training speedup, simple model, and real-time rendering capabilities.

On-device rendering

These videos were recorded from Virtual / Mixed Reality headset with color passthrough capabilities. We render reconstructed humans from ZJU-MoCap dataset with our custom shaders using TensoRF representation. Animation is done with linear blend skinning.
